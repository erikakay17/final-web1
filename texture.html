Texture and how it's made

Texture is the way different sounds are layered on top of each other. It's what tells you immediately that you're hearing EDM, or jazz, or any other genre. Most textures have a balance of different timbres, or types of sounds. Below are some examples of common textures, and the timbres that create them.

The Beatles

A drum set, with different kinds of drums, plus some cymbals, creates a texture all by itself. The drummer plays patterns that give us a strong idea of how fast the music goes. It's the sound we dance to. Then there are low guitars (bass guitars) and higher guitars. The guitars use pitches to make phrases of music that give us a feeling of whether the song is fun, serious, relaxed, joyful, etc. Then the singers add their own pitches, plus language.

Who else uses this texture?

BTS, The Supremes, Taylor Swift, Lizzo, Dua Lipa

Skrillex

This music is called EDM (electronic dance music) because all of the sounds are electronic. A studio musician played every note on an instrument to make a sample library and then those samples were made into tracks that arrange them in certain patterns. When Skrillex performs, he uses different types of tracks to create the sound you hear. There's always a beat, or a track that has repetitive sounds that you'll want to dance to. Then there's a buildup or breakdown, that uses different pitches and timbres to build excitement. The most intense set of sounds is the drop, and there will be tons of tracks overlapping when the drop happens. It's kind of like the high point of the song, and there can be lots of drops.

Who else uses this texture?

Deadmau5, Avicci

Movie Music

Pitch is the character of the sounds we hear that make us say it's high or low or something in between. Pitches are grouped together to make chords.
